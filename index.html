Tension Log

Structural tensions where the system is evolving, splitting, or carrying unresolved load. This is a meta‑level view, not a design document.

Tension
Governance vs Exploration
  • Need for strict guardrails vs appetite for high‑novelty scans.
  • Vara:Scan pushes edges; Vault and Constitution pull toward stability.
Tension
Compression vs Ambiguity
  • Vault compresses patterns; narrative surfaces preserve ambiguity.
  • Too much compression risks losing nuance; too little risks overload.
Tension
Portability vs Substrate Drift
  • Design aims to be substrate‑agnostic.
  • Different AI systems exert pressure on how invariants are implemented.
Tension
Operator Agency vs Guardrails
  • Operators need freedom to act; system needs to prevent harmful paths.
  • Playbooks and manuals mediate this tension in practice.
Use: This log is meant as a thinking surface for readers like Caruso—places to ask “what is this becoming?” without needing access to internals.

Hypothesis Ledger

Provisional reads on what the Lattice might be, structurally. These are hypotheses, not canonical claims.

Hypothesis
Governance Architecture
  • Lattice as a governance‑first architecture for hybrid human‑AI decisions.
  • Modules exist primarily to enforce and express governance invariants.
Hypothesis
Orientation Engine
  • Lattice as a system for orienting operators in constraint‑dense environments.
  • Vara, Vault, and narrative surfaces act as orientation instruments.
Hypothesis
Governed Operating Environment
  • Lattice as a substrate‑independent operating environment for governed action.
  • Execution spine turns signal into action without losing reversibility.
Important: Vault‑Lite intentionally keeps these hypotheses open. The goal is not to “solve” the corpus, but to see what it is becoming when all surfaces are held together.
